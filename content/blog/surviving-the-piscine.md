## The Deep End

The 42 Piscine is notorious. It's a 26-day intensive coding bootcamp where you are given minimal instruction, a strict norm (style guide), and impossible deadlines. You code in C, and you only use Vim or Emacs.

## The Real Lesson

While I learned a tremendous amount about pointers, memory allocation, and data structures, the real lesson of the Piscine wasn't technical. It was psychological.

When you are placed in an environment where you are guaranteed to fail daily, you quickly shed your ego. You learn how to read man pages instead of relying on tutorials. Most importantly, you learn how to ask peers for help, and how to clearly explain complex concepts to others.

The Piscine teaches you how to learn. Once you know how to learn efficiently, any new framework or language—whether it's Next.js, Rust, or Python—is just syntax.
